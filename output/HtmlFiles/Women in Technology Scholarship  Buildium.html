Women in Technology Scholarship

Meg Whitman. Mary Barra. Virginia Rometty. Susan Wojcicki. Melinda Gates.

Five of the most powerful STEM leaders in the world. All of them women. All of them breaking boundaries for future female leaders, engineers, designers, creative directors, product developers, and CEOs.

And, all of them an inspiration to support female leaders in technology.

Here are some more fast facts about female leaders:

  • Women earn 60% of all bachelor degrees, 60% of all master’s degrees, 48% of all medical degrees, and a startling 18% of all STEM degrees.
  • 59% of the college-educated, entry-level workforce are women.
  • Only 4.6% of all Fortune 500 CEOs are female.
  • Only 24% of all STEM positions are held by women; only 9% of those women hold leadership positions.

You could probably find a lot of research to explain why women earn more degrees but fewer CEO positions. But what we want to know is how to balance the equation.

And, if you’re a female studying science, technology, engineering, or math (STEM), you probably have an answer. Which is why we’re proud to award one $2,500 scholarship each semester to a women who can tell us which female leader most inspires them to pursue their dreams, and why.

Think you’re up to the challenge? Read on to learn more about submission deadlines and requirements.

*As of Fall 2017, we introduced a second part of the submission process: video supplement. Both an essay and video are required for all submissions.

Essay Prompt:

In less than 1,000 words, tell us which female leader at a technology company inspires you, and why. She can be a CEO, a product developer, a creative director, an engineer, or anything in between. But she must be a leader in STEM.

Submission Guidelines:

  • All essays can be submitted to scholarship@buildium.com with a subject line of “Women in Technology Scholarship – [Your Name].”
  • Please attach them as a Word document or PDF. Essays submitted in the email body cannot be processed.
  • Please also attach a copy of your unofficial transcript as proof of your enrollment. If you are currently enrolled in your first semester, please send a copy of your acceptance letter. No other alternative forms of documentation will be accepted. 
  • In the body of your email, please include the following exactly:
    • First Name Last Name
    • Email address
    • Phone number
    • The school you are attending.
    • Your major/concentration
    • Expected Graduation year

Video Supplement:

Please record a short video in response to this question: “What would the Buildium Women in Technology Scholarship mean for your education?”

Video Guidelines:

  • Video entries must be 1-2 minutes in length and can be recorded using a smartphone.
  • ​Please state your name in the video and repeat the prompt when answering the question. Example: “Hi, my name is Lauren Mason. Winning the Buildium Women in Technology Scholarship would mean that ​I am able to start next semester with a little less stress knowing that my book fees are covered and…”
  • If shot using a smartphone, ​please record it in landscape (not portrait) mode. Phone should be held longways/horizontally. ​
  • Be CREATIVE and make it personal! ​You can talk from the heart, do a skit, ​do a demonstration, walk us through your school or classrooms—anything!

To be considered fully eligible, video submissions must meet the following criteria:

  • Videos must answer the prompt listed above. “What would the Buildium Women in Technology Scholarship mean for your education?”​
  • Videos must be original and should not include musical overlay.​
  • Videos can be sent as a link (if stored on YouTube or Vimeo), emailed with your essay submission if the file size is allowed, or it can be ​sent separately as a TEXT MESSAGE to the following email: scholarship@buildium.com. Please remember to include your name! 🙂

Scholarship Qualifications:

You must be a female enrolled in an accredited undergraduate or graduate degree program in the United States or Canada, studying Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM).

Applicants must be 18 years of age or older at the date of submission and in good academic standing with their college or university. We cannot accept applications from high school students, regardless of age. This scholarship is only for students CURRENTLY enrolled and actively studying in an undergraduate or graduate level program.

Submission Deadlines:

Our Fall 2019 Scholarship opens on October 1st and closes on November 1st. We will begin accepting applications again for our Spring scholarship rotation on March 15, 2020 and it closes on April 15, 2020.

**We operate under the same cycle every year.**

Please email us at scholarship@buildium.com with any other questions, and we’ll get back to you as soon as we can.

Applications submitted outside of the 1 month acceptance window will not be read or considered.
